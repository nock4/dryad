# Detroit Vacant Land Context

## Scale
- 100,000+ vacant lots (18 sq mi of vacancy in a 142.9 sq mi city)
- DLBA inventory: 59,617 lots (down from 67K in 2016, sold 28,801)
- City P&DD: 5,381 lots (commercial parcels)
- Private owners: 41,685 lots
- City spends $6.72M/year on mowing ($13.44/lot × 5 cuts × 100K lots)
- $456M spent demolishing 27,000 homes since 2014. Only 942 structures remain.

## DLBA Programs (buildingdetroit.org)
- Side Lot: $100, must own adjacent property
- Own It Now: non-adjacent lots at market rate ($1K-5K)
- Create-a-Project: $250/lot for LLC or 501(c)(3)
- Contact: LBP@detroitmi.gov, office hours 1st Thursday/month 12-1pm
- Must be current on all city payments
- Neighbor notification required 30 days before site work (≤1 acre)

## Environmental Justice
- Tree canopy declined 23% since 1950
- Low-income neighborhoods have 30% less canopy
- 19% of remaining canopy over unprotected vacant land
- Only 20% of parkland is natural areas (vs 57% national median)
- 286 of 875 block groups below 75 on Tree Equity Score
- Detroit Tree Equity Partnership: 75,000 trees, $30M, 300 jobs

## Chadsey-Condon Neighborhood
- Location: Southwest Detroit, 48216
- Demographics: Latino and African American community
- Vacancy: Among highest in city
- Strong community organizations, active neighborhood association

## DFC "Saving for the Future" (October 2025)
- Calls for creation of Detroit GreenSpace Conservancy
- Focus on natural areas over traditional parks — greater ecological services, lower maintenance
- Conservation easements for permanent protection
- Michigan Qualified Conservation Organizations get property tax exemptions
- Recreational Land Use Statute shields landowners from liability

## Property Taxes
- ~$30/lot/year for vacant land
- Detroit accepts crypto via PayPal at checkout (ipn4.paymentus.com)
- Proposed Land Value Tax: $30 → $67/lot, BUT urban farms/community gardens/designated community spaces are EXEMPT
- Michigan QCOs are fully tax-exempt on conservation land

## Impact Data (Philadelphia Study)
- 63% reduction in poor mental health near greened lots
- 37% reduction in crime perception
- 76% increase in outdoor recreation
- 5% property value increase within 500ft
- Tree canopy lowers temperatures up to 21°F
