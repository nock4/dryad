### Current Site Conditions

The nine lots are former residential parcels acquired through the Detroit Land Bank Authority. Structures have been demolished. Current vegetation is predominantly mowed turf grass with invasive species encroachment:

- **Tree of Heaven** (Ailanthus altissima) — multiple mature specimens and root suckers
- **Common Buckthorn** (Rhamnus cathartica) — hedgerow establishment along lot boundaries
- **Spotted Knapweed** (Centaurea stoebe) — scattered populations in disturbed soil areas

Soil is urban fill over native glacial till, with moderate compaction from demolition activity. No known contamination (Phase I not yet completed; typical for DLBA vacant lots in this area).

### Restoration Goals

**Primary objective:** Remove invasive species and establish a self-sustaining native plant community that provides habitat for Great Lakes migratory birds, declining pollinator species, and urban wildlife.

**Target ecological community:** Native mesic prairie/meadow with pollinator gardens and bird habitat shrub borders, reflecting pre-development vegetation patterns for this region of southeast Michigan.

**Specific habitat targets:**

1. **Pollinator meadow** (interior, ~60% of area) — Native wildflower and grass mix emphasizing species that support monarch butterflies, native bees, and other declining pollinators. Species: Black-eyed Susan, Wild Bergamot, Purple Coneflower, Butterfly Milkweed, Little Bluestem, Prairie Dropseed.

2. **Bird habitat border** (perimeter, ~30% of area) — Native shrub and small tree plantings providing nesting, foraging, and cover for migratory songbirds. Species: Serviceberry, Nannyberry Viburnum, Red Osier Dogwood, American Elderberry, Winterberry Holly.

3. **Soil stabilization / transition zones** (~10% of area) — Native ground cover for pathways and lot edges. Species: Pennsylvania Sedge, Wild Strawberry, Creeping Phlox.

### Implementation Plan

**Year 1 — Site Preparation & Invasive Removal**
- Cut and treat Tree of Heaven (hack-and-squirt with triclopyr)
- Remove Buckthorn (cut-stump treatment)
- Spot-treat Knapweed populations
- Soil amendment and light grading as needed
- Install temporary erosion control

**Year 1–2 — Planting & Establishment**
- Fall/spring seeding of native meadow mix
- Plug planting of key pollinator species
- Install native shrub borders
- Establish watering protocol for first growing season

**Years 2–5 — Establishment Management**
- Prescribed mowing (2x/year) to suppress weeds during establishment
- Spot treatment of invasive regrowth
- Supplemental planting as needed based on monitoring data
- Annual photo documentation and species surveys

**Years 5–10+ — Mature Management**
- Reduce mowing to 1x/year or transition to prescribed burn rotation
- Ongoing invasive species monitoring and rapid response
- Biodiversity monitoring via iNaturalist community science program
- Annual reporting to USFWS
